Here is a breakdown of the different colors you might see:

### ☀️ During the Day (The Blue)
*   **Blue:** This is the typical color you see on a clear, sunny day. This is due to a process called **Rayleigh scattering**, where the Earth's atmosphere scatters shorter wavelengths of sunlight (blue and violet) more effectively than longer wavelengths (red and orange).

### 🌅 At Sunrise and Sunset (The Warm Tones)
*   **Red, Orange, Pink, Yellow:** When the sun is near the horizon, its light has to travel through much more of the Earth's atmosphere. During this long journey, most of the blue light gets scattered away, leaving the longer wavelengths-the reds, oranges, and yellows-to reach your eyes.

### ☁️ When the Weather Changes
*   **Gray or White:** If it is heavily overcast, the clouds spread out the sunlight, making the sky appear a uniform, muted gray or white.
*   **Deep Indigo/Dark Blue:** Sometimes, just before a storm, the sky can take on a very deep, dramatic blue or indigo color.
